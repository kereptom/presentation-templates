// how it works

Usability testing in brief.

You watch real people use the thing before you ship.

  • Recruit. Five users who match the target segment.
  • Task. Give them a real goal, not a tour.
  • Observe. Where they pause, slip and recover.
  • Fix. The top friction before the next round.

// two paths

Assume, or ask.

Same feature, two approaches and two risk curves.

AssumeWe build on a hunch and hope it lands. Cheap to start, costly to be wrong.
SurveyWe ask at scale but only learn what we thought to ask.
InterviewWe talk to a few users in depth and hear the why.
TestWe watch them use it and see where it breaks.

What to use when.

Six common building blocks of a research practice.

InterviewsDeep one-on-one talks to hear motivations.
Usability testsWatch users attempt real tasks.
SurveysQuantify attitudes across a wide group.
AnalyticsSee what users actually do at scale.
Card sortingLearn how users group and label things.
Diary studiesCapture behavior over days, not minutes.

// what to keep in mind

Three rules of research.

Simple, but easy to forget under deadline.

  • Ask why. Behavior beats opinion: watch what they do.
  • Stay neutral. Leading questions only confirm your bias.
  • Share early. Findings nobody sees change nothing.
